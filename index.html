Juice WRLD

Juice WRLD, born Jarad Anthony Higgins on December 2, 1998, in Chicago, Illinois, was an American rapper, singer, and songwriter. He rose to fame in the late 2010s with his emotional and melodic style that blended rap with rock and emo influences. His breakout hit, “Lucid Dreams” (2018), became a global success and was praised for its raw honesty about heartbreak and mental struggles. Juice WRLD was known for his freestyle abilities and his deeply personal lyrics, often addressing mental health, addiction, and the pain of relationships. His vulnerability and authenticity resonated with millions of young listeners who saw their own struggles reflected in his music. History

Early Life

  • Born: December 2, 1998, in Chicago, Illinois, USA.
  • Juice WRLD's real name was Jarad Anthony Higgins.
  • In high school, he began uploading songs to SoundCloud under the name "JuiceTheKidd" later changing it to Juice WRLD — inspired by the movie Juice starring Tupac Shakur.

Struggles and Death

  • Juice WRLD openly spoke about his battles with anxiety, depression, and drug addiction.
  • He died on December 8, 2019, at just 21 years old, after suffering a seizure at Chicago’s Midway Airport.

Legacy

  • Juice WRLD is remembered as a generational voice, especially among young people dealing with emotional struggles.
  • His music continues to influence the emo-rap and melodic trap scene, helping to destigmatize mental health issues in hip-hop.

ERA Good Bye & Good Riddance

The "Goodbye & Good Riddance" era refers to the time surrounding the release of Juice WRLD's debut studio album, Goodbye & Good Riddance, which dropped on May 23, 2018. This was a pivotal moment in his career, marking his breakthrough into the mainstream. The origin of Goodbye & Good Riddance lies in Juice WRLD’s personal experiences with heartbreak, drug use, and emotional struggles as a teenager and young adult.

All Girls are the Same

“All Girls Are the Same” flips the rap stereotype of an artist conquering a plethora of women with a Juice WRLD signature – a slow moving track about sorrow and heartbreak. Cole Bennett and Lyrical Lemonade helped this track gain major popularity with the music video

Lucid Dreams

Lucid dreaming refers to a state of consciousness where a person is aware they are dreaming. With enough experience, a dreamer can slowly begin to control the contents of their dreams. Juice uses the idea of controlling a lucid dream as an analogy for his mental state.

ERA Legends Never Die

The "Legends Never Die" era represents the posthumous tribute to Juice WRLD’s legacy following his tragic death in December 2019. The album Legends Never Die was released on July 10, 2020, and became one of the most successful posthumous albums in modern music history.

LEGEND

Juice WRLD is considered a legend by many due to his profound impact on modern music, particularly within the rap and emo-rap genres. His ability to blend rap with emo and melodic elements created a unique sound that resonated with millions, especially younger audiences.


Known for his raw, vulnerable lyrics, Juice WRLD tackled topics like emotional pain, heartbreak, mental health struggles, and substance abuse, which made him a voice for many who felt unheard. Before his tragic death in 2019 at just 21, Juice WRLD was incredibly prolific, releasing multiple albums and tracks that showcased his creativity and quick improvisation skills. His ability to freestyle and create songs in minutes highlighted his genius. His legacy lives on through his music, inspiring a new generation of listeners and artists.
